Careers At Programmers 4 Peace

Global Partnership and Grant Writing Internship (Unpaid)

About Programmers 4 Peace

Programmers 4 Peace is focused on creating peace through opportunity. We want to make technology education available world wide so that the future generation has the power and knowledge to get online, access a digital economy and solve their own problems using tech.

About You

You are a recent univeristy grad in either international development, or a business related field. You are looking to gain experience in the not for profit industry. You are a self starter with excellent attention to detail. You believe that technology has the power to change lives.

Primary Responsibilities and Objectives

Programmers 4 Peace is in search of a Global Partnership and Grant Writing Intern to manage several grant-related projects. Possible projects include designing a program to capture narrative stories of people who have been impacted by our programs. We are looking for pivotal stories that will help us to tell our funders about Programmers 4 Peace's impact. In addition, we are hoping to more effectively partner with organizations across the globe to create opportunities for access to tech education.

The position provides an excellent opportunity to gain experience in business writing/proposal writing and narrative storytelling. In addition, our intern will learn about full-cycle grant management, including application process, budgeting, scoping, and submitting a grant.

Our intern will also learn how to reach out to and form partnerships with existing tech companies. This is an amazing opportunity for networking.

Work Hours and Environment

This is a remote position

10 - 20 hours per week, flexible schedule.

The opportunity to be part of a remote team of change makers

What We Expect

A strong work ethic

A passion for problem solving

A can do attitude

A willingness to learn

How to apply:

To apply please send your resume to: hello at brittanyhemming.com. Please use the subject "Application for Global Partner and Grant Writing Internship"

In your cover letter please tell us about who you are, your experience, why you are interested in this role and why you think you would be the ideal candidate for this position. We would also like you to tell us how you got involved with technology and why you think coding education is important.

Please note that this is an unpaid internship, however you will have access to all course materials and one on one mentoring sessions to learn to code.
